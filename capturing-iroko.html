The Archive · Visual Holdings

Visual Ethnography

Délé Fágbèmí &Ọ̀;. maintains an ongoing visual ethnography portfolio documenting sacred spaces, ritual practice, and community life across the Afro-Atlantic world. These images serve as both primary source documentation and a form of engaged scholarship, recording material culture, built environments, and ceremonial contexts that rarely enter formal archival collections. Fieldwork sites include Havana, Matanzas, and Pinar del Río, Cuba, with ongoing documentation in the United States and broader Afro-Atlantic diaspora.

All photographs © Délé Fágbèmí &Ọ̀;, on loan to Iroko Historical Society. Reproduction or use without written authorization is prohibited. Access to restricted materials is governed by the Society's six-tier access framework.

What the Collection Documents

Cuba · Since 2002

Havana · Matanzas · Pinar del Río

Fieldwork documentation across more than two decades, including sacred spaces, ceremonial contexts, and material culture across western Cuba.

Sacred Architecture

Ilés & Ritual Spaces

Documentary photographs of consecrated spaces, altars, shrines, and the built environments of Afro-Atlantic sacred practice.

Ritual Practice

Ceremony & Observance

Visual records of ceremonial contexts authorized for documentation, with access governed by community protocols specific to each body of images.

Material Culture

Sacred Objects & Regalia

Documentation of material objects, regalia, and instruments including sacred drum sets and votive materials across traditions.

Community Life

Practitioners & Community

Portraiture and community documentation from religious communities across Cuba and the Afro-Atlantic diaspora, subject to individual access authorizations.

Foundation Day

IHS Annual Documentation

Annual visual documentation of the Society's Foundation Day observance and associated ceremonial activities.

Access note: The Ethnographique archive contains both publicly viewable and restricted materials.
