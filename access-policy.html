Access & Use Policy

The Iroko Historical Society is committed to providing meaningful access to its archival holdings while honoring the cultural, spiritual, and ethical dimensions of the materials in its care. This policy outlines the principles and procedures governing access to the Society's collections.

General Principles

Access to archival materials is provided on the basis of legitimate research need, subject to the ethical and custodial constraints appropriate to each collection. The Society recognizes that many of its holdings contain sacred, initiatory, or culturally sensitive content that requires governance beyond standard archival access protocols.

Access Tiers

The Society employs a six-tier access governance model, consistent with the Iroko Framework's field-level access control:

  • Public-Unrestricted — Open to all without restriction
  • Public-Contextual — Open with contextual framing provided
  • Researcher-Verified — Available to credentialed researchers upon application
  • Community-Restricted — Available to verified community members
  • Initiated-Only — Restricted to initiated practitioners
  • Initiated-Elder — Restricted to senior initiated authorities

Requesting Access

Researchers and practitioners may request access to materials by contacting the Society through the contact page. Requests should include a description of the research purpose, relevant credentials or community affiliations, and the specific collections or materials of interest.

Reproduction and Citation

Reproduction of archival materials is subject to review and may require custodial or community authorization. Published use of materials accessed through the Society must include appropriate citation and, where applicable, acknowledgment of community provenance.

Ongoing Review

This policy is subject to periodic review as the Society develops its infrastructure and expands its holdings. Updates will be posted to this page.
