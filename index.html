What We Do

The Iroko Historical Society stewards archival collections related to Africana religious practice, ritual knowledge, ethnobotany, and community history. Our holdings reflect forms of knowledge that have circulated outside formal institutional archives for centuries and require culturally informed stewardship.

The Society is structured as a formal archival institution supported through institutional grants and strategic partnerships, with stewardship guided by documented access and preservation policies.

Access Philosophy

Public access is provided through collection-level descriptions and finding aids. Item-level access to sensitive materials is governed by ethical review, custodial permission, and community consultation. This staged approach ensures that visibility does not come at the expense of cultural integrity.

Ethical Governance

Six-tier access permissions from public-unrestricted through initiated-elder, applied per property across all collections.

Community Authority

Communities retain authority over their knowledge. The archive serves the tradition, not the institution.

Semantic Infrastructure

The Iroko Framework provides 16 modular vocabularies for interoperable description across seven language families.

Postcustodial Design

Vocabulary publication (CC0) is separated from data publication (API-governed, community-authorized).

Contribute

The Society welcomes inquiries about material donations, research access, and institutional support. We are actively building capacity to accept donations of archival materials, including personal libraries, manuscript notes, photographs, and digital media.

If you hold materials relevant to Africana religious practice and cultural heritage, we invite you to contact us to discuss potential contributions.
